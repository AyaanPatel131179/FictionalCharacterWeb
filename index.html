Jon Snow

Jon Snow, initially believed to be the illegitimate son (bastard) of Eddard Stark, the Lord of Winterfell, and raised at Winterfell alongside his legitimate children, has a more complex family background. His true parentage is revealed to be far more significant than first assumed. Jon is the son of Lyanna Stark, Eddard's younger sister, and Rhaegar Targaryen, the crown prince of the Targaryen dynasty. This makes Jon a legitimate heir to the Targaryen throne, as his birth was kept secret to protect him from Robert Baratheon’s rebellion, which sought to overthrow the Targaryen family. His full name, Aegon Targaryen, reflects his connection to this powerful and noble lineage. Thus, Jon Snow's true family line links him both to the ancient Stark house of the North and the royal Targaryen bloodline of the Seven Kingdoms.

  1. The Wildlings (Free Folk): Initially tasked with defending the Wall, Jon later fought alongside the Wildlings during key battles like Hardhome.
  2. Night’s Watch Mutineers: After becoming Lord Commander, Jon fought against fellow members who rebelled against his leadership, culminating in his victory over them.
  3. Ramsay Bolton: In the Battle of the Bastards, Jon led the North's forces to defeat the sadistic Ramsay and reclaim Winterfell.
  4. The White Walkers and the Night King: Jon’s most significant fight was against the White Walkers, culminating in the Battle of Winterfell, where he helped defeat the Night King and save humanity.
  5. The Bolton Forces: Before the Battle of the Bastards, Jon fought against the Boltons to free Winterfell from their control.
